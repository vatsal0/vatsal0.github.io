Slow Learning

July 3, 2026

Back to writing

If you try to learn something too quickly, you won't learn it at all. Learning requires a degree of slowness, so it cannot be directly optimized. It may take X number of days for your brain to fully grasp a subject, and while some interventions like improving your study habits might help, there will be a minimum amount of time required for you to learn. This was a very difficult realization for me, but I think being aware of it helps immensely with time management. If I tried to cram in some new knowledge in X-1 days, I would prevent myself from actually grasping the material and those X-1 days would effectively be wasted. So spending that extra day is actually the right choice in order to maximize the effectiveness of time spent learning.
